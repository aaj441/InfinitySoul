## Mission

The RAWKUS Ethics Lab is a **501(c)(3) non-profit research foundation** dedicated to:
- Advancing ethical AI in insurance and risk management
- Training underrepresented security practitioners
- Funding open-source protocol research
- Ensuring community governance over AI deployment

**The model**: **10% of all RAWKUS AI LLC net revenue** funds the lab in perpetuity.

## Governance: House Committee

The Ethics Lab is governed by a **House Committee** with distributed voting power:

| Member | Vote Weight | Role |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Telluride Alum #1 | 20% | A&R #1 (community advocate) |
| Telluride Alum #2 | 20% | A&R #2 (practitioner perspective) |
| AI Researcher #1 | 15% | Producer #1 (technical oversight) |
| AI Researcher #2 | 15% | Producer #2 (ethics oversight) |
| Policyholder Representative | 10% | The crowd (user voice) |
| Security Practitioner | 10% | Street team (operational insight) |
| Founder (You) | 10% | Label head (tie-breaker only) |

**Quorum**: 40% of voting power must be present  
**Threshold**: 60% approval required for decisions  
**Cadence**: Weekly meetings (A&Rs meet every week to sign artists)

## Programs

### 1. Fellowship Program

**Budget**: $1M/year  
**Structure**: 10 fellows × $100K/year  
**Duration**: 1-year renewable fellowships  
**Focus Areas**:
- Ethical AI in insurance
- Open-source risk modeling
- Community governance protocols
- Adversarial security testing

**Selection Criteria**:
- Demonstrated commitment to open-source
- Track record in security or AI ethics
- Community endorsement (Discord votes)
- Alignment with RAWKUS principles

**Examples**:
- @hacker_on_probation (ethical exploit research)
- @union_ai (labor rights in AI deployment)
- Former MGA founders (industry transformation)

### 2. Grant Program

**Budget**: $500K/year  
**Structure**: $10K-$50K grants for specific projects  
**Review Process**: House Committee votes quarterly  

**Eligible Projects**:
- Open-source insurance protocols
- Ethical constraint engines
- Community governance tools
- Security practitioner training programs

### 3. Bounty Program

**Budget**: $200K/year  
**Structure**: Rewards for finding ethical violations or security vulnerabilities  

**Bounty Categories**:
- **Ethical Violations**: $5K-$25K for identifying biased or unfair agent behavior
- **Security Exploits**: $10K-$50K for responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities
- **Protocol Improvements**: $1K-$10K for accepted protocol enhancements

**Submission**: Via Discord #bounty-submissions channel

### 4. Publications Program

**Budget**: $0 (volunteer-driven)  
**Format**: Raw markdown, no gatekeepers  
**Distribution**: GitHub, Discord, Twitter/X  

**Philosophy**: 
- No academic journals (too slow, too gatekept)
- No peer review gatekeeping (community review instead)
- Public from day one (no embargoes)
- CC-BY-SA licensed (open forever)

**Example Publications**:
- "The Rawkus Bar: Ethical Constraints as Lyrical Integrity"
- "30-Second Underwriting: Boom-Bap for Insurance"
- "Community Governance: Token Votes as A&Rs"

### 5. Training Program

**Budget**: Funded via HoldCo operations  
**Model**: Free security training (Telluride model)  
**Goal**: Train 1,000 liberated practitioners who can never be priced out again  

**Curriculum**:
- Ethical AI deployment
- Open-source risk modeling
- Community governance participation
- Adversarial security testing

## The Indie Philosophy

The RAWKUS Ethics Lab operates on **indie label principles**:

1. **Artists own their work**: Fellows and grant recipients retain all IP rights
2. **No gatekeepers**: Publications are raw markdown, community-reviewed
3. **Community A&Rs**: Discord votes on grants, not bureaucratic committees
4. **Major label money, indie soul**: 10% tithe from HoldCo funds the lab
5. **Open-source forever**: All research is CC-BY-SA licensed
