## Ten Epic Questions a JPL-Level Systems Architect Would Ask

### 1. What's the orbital mechanics of human behavior?

JPL doesn't launch rockets by guessing. They model gravitational fields, fuel consumption, trajectory windows. **What are the equivalent forces acting on human wellness?**

Music isn't random emotional noise — it's a **forcing function**. Tempo is gravitational pull. Valence is escape velocity. We're not building a playlist app. We're building a **behavioral trajectory system** where the intervention (music) is the thrust vector and the outcome (hydration, exercise, sleep) is the orbital insertion.

### 2. Where's the telemetry?

Every JPL mission has continuous telemetry. **What's our signal-to-noise ratio on human behavior change?**

Last.fm gives us 20+ years of passive behavioral telemetry — millions of data points you didn't consciously record. That's not a music history. That's a **longitudinal study of one** that rivals anything in clinical psychology. The intervention tracker closes the loop: stimulus → response → outcome → refinement.

### 3. What's the minimum viable constellation?

SpaceX doesn't launch one Starlink satellite. They launch constellations that create network effects. **What's our constellation?**

- Node 1: Music listening (Last.fm, Spotify)
- Node 2: Health behaviors (self-report, wearables)
- Node 3: Outcomes (insurance claims, productivity metrics)
- Node 4: Interventions (playlists, nudges, coaching)

Each node alone is just data. Together, they're a **closed-loop behavioral operating system**.

### 4. What's the failure mode we're designing against?

Lockheed Martin engineers ask: "What kills the mission?" **What kills behavior change?**

- **Shame**: "You failed your diet again" → We never judge, only observe
- **Friction**: "Open 3 apps to log a glass of water" → We infer from what you already do
- **Abstraction**: "Eat 2000 calories" → We say "here's a playlist that makes cooking feel good"

We're designing for **the ADHD brain**. If it takes willpower, it will fail. If it's automatic, it scales.

### 5. What's the propellant we're not seeing?

The Saturn V's first stage was mostly fuel. **What's the hidden energy source in this system?**

It's **identity**. People don't change behaviors — they change who they believe they are. Music is identity fuel. "I'm the kind of person who listens to lo-fi while meal prepping" is more powerful than "I should cook more." We're not selling interventions. We're **selling upgraded self-concepts** that happen to come with better health outcomes.

### 6. How do we survive the valley of death?

Every startup, every JPL mission, has a phase where everything looks like it's failing. **What's our survival architecture?**

Zero-dollar bootstrapping → Vercel Pro + Railway. First revenue from **micro-transactions on intervention data** sold back to insurers. Not "we'll figure out monetization later." We know exactly who pays: the people who pay when behavior change fails (insurers, employers, healthcare systems).

### 7. What's the 10x version we're not building yet?

Elon asks: "What would make this 10x better?" **What's ours?**

Right now: Music → Behavior
10x version: **Any stimulus → Any behavior**

The WellnessGenome engine is stimulus-agnostic. It could map:
- Podcast listening → productivity
- Color exposure → mood
- Social interaction patterns → mental health
- Sleep sounds → recovery quality

Music is just the **proof of concept** for a universal behavioral physics engine.

### 8. Who's the unexpected customer?

Bill Gates didn't sell to consumers first — he sold to IBM. **Who's our IBM?**

- **Life insurers**: They pay $500K+ on preventable claims. A 5% reduction in claims = millions saved
- **Pharma**: Medication non-adherence costs $528B/year. Music-based reminders could capture 1% of that
- **Universities**: Student mental health is a crisis. They're desperate for scalable interventions
- **Workers' comp**: Behavioral health claims are exploding. Music-based return-to-work programs

The individual user is the product. The B2B buyer is the customer.

### 9. What's the moat?

Amazon has logistics. Google has data. **What do we have that can't be copied?**

**20 years of your personal behavioral telemetry.** No competitor can replicate your Last.fm history. No AI can synthesize the specific patterns of YOUR life. The moat is **biographical data that takes a lifetime to accumulate**.

Plus: every intervention we run makes the model smarter. First-mover advantage compounds.

### 10. What happens when this works?

Robin Williams' character in Good Will Hunting saw the kid's potential when no one else did. **If this works, what does the world look like?**

- Insurance becomes **proactive**, not reactive
- Healthcare shifts from **treatment** to **trajectory management**
- "Mental health" becomes as measurable as blood pressure
- People have a **personal behavioral API** they own and control
- Music isn't entertainment — it's **infrastructure for human flourishing**
