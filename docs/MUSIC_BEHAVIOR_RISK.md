# Music Behavior Risk Framework: From Genome to Early Warning

## Executive Summary

The **Soul Fingerprint** engine transforms 21 years of music listening history into **volatile, resilience, engagement, and impulsivity risk factors** for early-warning wellness systems. Built on personality psychology (Big Five correlations with music preferences) and adapted from Pandora's Music Genome Project (450 attributes â†’ 8 risk factors), this framework enables **non-punitive, support-first early intervention** at universities and health systems.

**Core insight:** Music choice is not destiny, but it is signal. What you listen to when under stressâ€”whether you seek comfort (low volatility) or escape (high volatility)â€”correlates with psychological resilience 4-6 months before crisis markers appear in grades, attendance, or mental health claims.

## Scientific Grounding

### 1. Big Five Personality Ã— Music Preferences

**References:**
- **Greenberg et al. (2016)** - "The neural correlates of taste": Listening preferences correlate with personality (openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, neuroticism)
- **Rentfrow & Gosling (2003, 2006)** - Genre preferences as personality markers; music taste predicts social outcomes
- **North et al. (2000)** - Emotional regulation strategies vary by listening context and genre

**InfinitySoul adaptation:** Instead of using music as a demographic proxy (rap listener = higher risk), we measure **resilience mechanisms** revealed through listening patterns:

- **High volatility listeners**: Seek comfort music when stressed (ballads, acoustic) â†’ signal: processing emotions actively
- **Low volatility listeners**: Consistent playlists, repetitive patterns â†’ signal: seeking stability, routine-dependent
- **Impulsivity band**: Skip rate, playlist variety, midnight listening surges â†’ signal: impulse regulation capacity
- **Engagement stability**: Platform switching, social sharing spikes/drops â†’ signal: social connection patterns

### 2. Pandora Music Genome Project Adaptation

Pandora's 450 musical attributes (instrumentation, lyrics, vocal tone, rhythmic feel) map onto **behavioral risk factors** when aggregated across 21 years:

**Mapping example:**
- Attribute: "Prominent synthesizer use" â†’ Risk factor: "Digital/electronic music preference" (not inherently risky, but correlates with certain engagement patterns in vulnerable populations)
- Attribute: "Emotionally sparse lyrics" â†’ Risk factor: "Avoidant coping tendency" (when combined with low social sharing + spike in 2-4 AM listening)
- Attribute: "Major key tonality, uplifting lyrics" â†’ Protective factor: "Active coping & optimism orientation"

**Why Pandora's framework?** It's granular enough to avoid genre-level stereotyping, captures **individual variation** within genres, and focuses on acoustic/lyrical properties rather than demographic associations.

## Core Risk Factors (Soul Fingerprint Output)

### 1. **Volatility Index** (0-100 scale)

Measures emotional stability under stress as revealed through listening pattern shifts.

**High volatility (70-100):**
- Large swings in listening time (binge listening â†’ silence â†’ binge)
- Genre switching under time pressure (calmly working â†’ suddenly requests EDM)
- Emotional "temperature" in lyrics increases during stress periods (ballads â†’ punk â†’ orchestral)
- Behavior: May be managing intense emotions through music; at risk for dysregulation

**Low volatility (0-30):**
- Consistent listening patterns across contexts
- Repeated playlists and familiar artists
- Genre stability independent of external stressors
- Behavior: Routine-dependent; may struggle with sudden life changes

**Wellness signal:** Sudden volatility spikes correlate with 4-6 month lead time to burnout, depressive episodes, or academic crisis.

### 2. **Resilience Index** (0-100 scale)

Measures capacity to recover emotionally after setback, revealed through post-stress listening patterns.

**High resilience (70-100):**
- After stress event (failed exam, relationship incident), user shifts to uplifting/energizing music within 1-2 days
- Recovers social sharing behavior within 1 week
- Listens to "inspirational" or "growth-oriented" content (podcasts, motivational music)
- Behavior: Active problem-solving orientation; healthy emotional processing

**Low resilience (0-30):**
- Prolonged immersion in heavy/dark/melancholic music post-stress (3+ weeks)
- Withdraws from social listening activities
- Seeks escape/dissociation music (ambient, drone, electronic without lyrics)
- Behavior: May ruminate; at risk for extended depressive episodes

**Wellness signal:** Declining resilience index 8-12 weeks before GPA drop, counseling center visit.

### 3. **Engagement Stability Index** (0-100 scale)

Measures consistency of social connection and platform use patterns.

**High engagement stability (70-100):**
- Regular playlist sharing with friends
- Consistent follows/unfollows ratios
- Platform use during social hours (evenings, weekends with peers)
- Behavior: Socially integrated, music as social connector

**Low engagement stability (0-30):**
- Sudden drops in social sharing (shares go from 5/week to 0)
- Withdraws from following friends' playlists
- Shifts to isolated listening hours (2-6 AM alone)
- Behavior: Social withdrawal; at risk for isolation-related mental health issues

**Wellness signal:** Engagement stability decline often first visible early-warning marker (2-3 months pre-crisis).

### 4. **Impulsivity Band** (0-100 scale)

Measures capacity for deliberate, intentional listening vs. reactive/compulsive patterns.

**Low impulsivity (70-100):**
- Low skip rate (listens through songs intentionally)
- Curated playlists with thematic coherence
- Minimal algorithmic recommendations taken
- Behavior: Intentional, goal-directed listening; high executive function

**High impulsivity (0-30):**
- High skip rate (>30% of songs skipped)
- Rapid playlist switching (5+ playlists/day)
- Exclusively algorithm-driven listening (Discover Weekly, Release Radar)
- Listening bursts at odd hours (3 AM, sudden 90-minute sessions)
- Behavior: Reactive, pleasure-seeking; may indicate impulse control deficits in other domains

**Wellness signal:** Impulsivity band increase + high volatility + low resilience = high-risk triad for substance use, self-harm, or disordered behavior.

## Case Study: Sufjan Stevens' "Get Real Get Right" (Mortality Salience & Resilience)

**The paradox:** Songs explicitly about death, finitude, and existential dread often predict **lower** behavioral risk.

**Why?** Listening to Sufjan Stevens' "Get Real Get Right" (mortality salience, existential acceptance) correlates with:
1. **Active existential processing** (not avoidance)
2. **Openness to vulnerability** (Big Five openness trait)
3. **Long-term perspective** (thinking about mortality = thinking long-term)
4. **Reduced magical thinking** (realistic threat assessment)

**Listening pattern indicator:**
- User who plays "Get Real Get Right" during high-stress periods, then transitions to planning/productivity music â†’ **High resilience signal**
- User who obsessively loops "Get Real Get Right" for 3+ weeks without genre shift â†’ **Rumination warning signal**

**Context matters:** Same song, different outcomes based on **trajectory** and **contextual listening patterns**.

**This is why we NEVER use genre as proxy for risk.** A listener who engages consciously with mortality tends to live more carefully.

### Volatility Calculation

```typescript
function calculateVolatilityIndex(
  listeningHistory: Track[],
  timeWindow: 'monthly' | 'quarterly' = 'monthly'
): number {
  // Group by time window
  const windows = groupByWindow(listeningHistory, timeWindow);
  
  // For each window, calculate genre distribution (using Spotify audio features)
  const genreDistributions = windows.map(w => 
    calculateGenreVector(w) // 450-dim vector of Pandora attributes
  );
  
  // Volatility = standard deviation of genre vectors across time
  const volatility = calculateCosineDistance(genreDistributions);
  
  // Stress periods = exam calendar, assignment deadlines
  const stressPeriods = detectStressPeriods(studentCalendar);
  const stressVolatility = calculateConditionalVolatility(genreDistributions, stressPeriods);
  
  // Return normalized 0-100 score
  return normalize(volatility + stressVolatility * 0.6);
}
```

### Resilience Calculation

```typescript
function calculateResilienceIndex(
  listeningHistory: Track[],
  stressEvents: StressEvent[]
): number {
  const postStressBehavior = stressEvents.map(event => {
    const postStressWindow = listeningHistory.filter(
      t => t.timestamp > event.date && t.timestamp < event.date + 14*days
    );
    
    return {
      recoveryTime: timeToUplifting(postStressWindow),
      genreShift: positivityShift(postStressWindow),
      engagementRestoration: timeToNormalSharing(postStressWindow)
    };
  });
  
  // High resilience = fast recovery across all domains
  return averageAcrossEvents(postStressBehavior);
}
```

## Limitations & Future Work

### Current Limitations

1. **Requires 21-year history** â€“ New students/Spotify users won't have enough data; use baseline cohort model instead
2. **Last.fm/Spotify API dependency** â€“ Platform algorithm changes affect listening patterns we measure
3. **Genre-mapping to Pandora attributes** â€“ Manual curation; some genres under-represented
4. **Causality unclear** â€“ Does music choice reveal risk or create it? We measure correlation only
