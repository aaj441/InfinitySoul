## ðŸ”¬ CHAPTER 8: METHODOLOGY AND VALIDATION

### **8.1 Statistical Rigor**

**Significance Testing:**
- **Null Hypothesis**: No correlation between music taste and behavioral risk
- **Alternative Hypothesis**: Significant correlation exists
- **Significance Level**: Î± = 0.05
- **Power Analysis**: 1-Î² = 0.80 minimum

**Effect Size Calculations:**
- **Cohen's d**: For mean differences
- **Pearson's r**: For correlations
- **Odds Ratios**: For categorical outcomes
- **Confidence Intervals**: 95% for all estimates

### **8.2 Cross-Validation Framework**

**Validation Strategies:**
1. **Holdout Validation**: 80/20 train/test split
2. **K-Fold Cross-Validation**: 10-fold for model selection
3. **External Validation**: Independent dataset testing
4. **Temporal Validation**: Future prediction validation

**Performance Metrics:**
- **Accuracy**: Overall correct classification rate
- **Precision**: True positives / (True positives + False positives)
- **Recall**: True positives / (True positives + False negatives)
- **F1 Score**: Harmonic mean of precision and recall
- **AUC-ROC**: Area under receiver operating characteristic curve

### **8.3 Bias Detection and Mitigation**

**Fairness Auditing:**
- **Demographic Parity**: Equal prediction rates across groups
- **Equalized Odds**: Equal true positive/false positive rates
- **Calibration**: Prediction accuracy consistent across groups
- **Individual Fairness**: Similar individuals receive similar treatment

**Bias Mitigation Strategies:**
1. **Pre-processing**: Data augmentation and balancing
2. **In-processing**: Algorithmic fairness constraints
3. **Post-processing**: Threshold optimization and calibration
